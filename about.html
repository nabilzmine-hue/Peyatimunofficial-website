Organisation Overview

PEYATIM is one of Malaysia's most established Islamic welfare organisations, providing safe homes and brighter futures for orphaned children since 1982.

Pertubuhan Kebajikan Anak-Anak Yatim Islam Malaysia (PEYATIM) is a registered non-governmental organisation (NGO) operating under the supervision of Jabatan Kebajikan Masyarakat (JKM), Malaysia. We are committed to providing comprehensive welfare, education, and spiritual guidance to orphaned and underprivileged Muslim children across the country.

With our headquarters in Kuala Lumpur and eight branch offices spanning Selangor, Pahang, Kelantan, Johor, Terengganu, Perak, Penang, and Sabah, PEYATIM's reach continues to grow. We currently care for more than 250 children across all facilities.

PEYATIM is fully funded through public donations including zakat, sadaqah, wakaf, and corporate sponsorships. Every ringgit donated goes directly to improving the lives of our children.

Our History

Humble Beginnings (1982)

PEYATIM was founded on 10 October 1982 by a group of dedicated Muslim community leaders who recognised the urgent need for a structured welfare system for orphaned children in Malaysia. The organisation began with a single rented house in Chow Kit, Kuala Lumpur, sheltering just 12 children with the help of 5 volunteers.

Growth and Recognition (1990s)

Throughout the 1990s, PEYATIM grew rapidly, acquiring its first permanent building in 1993 and receiving official recognition from the Ministry of Women, Family and Community Development. By 1998, the organisation had expanded to three states and was caring for over 80 children.

National Expansion (2000s – Present)

The 21st century saw PEYATIM transform into a nationally recognised welfare body. The establishment of the Tahfiz Al-Quran Programme in 2003, the opening of the Skills Training Centre in 2010, and the launch of the PEYATIM Education Trust Fund in 2015 have all contributed to a holistic model of child welfare considered a benchmark for Islamic orphanages in Southeast Asia.

Today, PEYATIM proudly counts more than 1,800 alumni who have graduated from our programmes and gone on to build successful careers as doctors, engineers, teachers, and entrepreneurs.

Mission, Vision & Objectives

🎯 Mission

To provide a safe, nurturing and Islamic environment for orphaned and underprivileged Muslim children, empowering them through quality education, spiritual development, and skills training so that they may become independent, contributing members of society.

💡 Vision

To be Malaysia's leading Islamic welfare organisation, recognised for excellence in the holistic development of orphaned children, and to set a regional benchmark in child welfare by 2030.

Our Objectives

  • To provide shelter, food, clothing, and basic necessities for orphaned children
  • To ensure every child receives formal education from primary to tertiary level
  • To instil Islamic values, Quranic knowledge, and good moral character
  • To equip children with vocational and life skills for self-sufficiency
  • To foster a sense of family, belonging, and emotional security
  • To raise public awareness and encourage community participation in child welfare
  • To manage donations transparently and channel funds efficiently to beneficiaries
